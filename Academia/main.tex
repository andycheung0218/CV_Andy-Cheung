\section{Research Output}
    \resumeSubHeadingListStart
        \resumeSubheading{Sentiment and Housing Policy Effectiveness in China: Evidence from a GPT-Driven Text Mining Approach}{Sep 2024 -- May 2025}{Advisor: Prof. Lu Yang}{Bachelor Honor Thesis}
        \resumeItemListStart
            \resumeItem{\textbf{Abstract:} This study investigates how public sentiment affects housing market policy effectiveness in China by introducing two methodological innovations. Firstly, it develops a GPT-driven text mining model to construct a housing market sentiment index from Weibo posts, achieving over 80\% classification accuracy while reducing technical barriers. Secondly, it employs a smooth transition local projection method to examine how policy transmission varies across sentiment growth and dispersion regimes. Using monthly data from 2019 to 2024, analysis reveals contractionary mortgage rate policies yield significantly different outcomes. During positive sentiment growth, a 100 basis point increase in mortgage rate leads to a 0.35 percentage point increase in housing price return after eight months. Similarly, during high sentiment dispersion, a 100 basis point increase in mortgage rate leads to a 0.26 percentage point increase in housing price return. Conversely, policies are effective during negative sentiment growth or low dispersion, with same policy reducing housing price return by 0.87 and 0.55 percentage point respectively. These differential effects become statistically significant 7-9 months after implementation and are robust. Findings suggest policymakers should consider sentiment dynamics, as conventional policies may be counterproductive under optimistic growth or high disagreement.}
            \resumeItem{\textbf{Keywords:} Text Mining, Sentiment Analysis, Housing Market Policy Effectiveness, Local Projection, Sentiment Dispersion}
